Stone Insights Blog

Marble in Architecture

Marble, a metamorphic stone derived from limestone, is prized for its veining and translucence. While it adds timeless elegance to interiors, it requires sealing and care due to its softness and porosity. Common uses include flooring, staircases, bathroom walls, and sculptures.

Granite: The Durable Choice

Granite forms from cooled magma and is one of the hardest natural stones. Its durability, stain resistance, and wide color range make it ideal for countertops, outdoor paving, and monuments. Little maintenance is needed beyond sealing for longevity.

Limestone forms in marine environments and offers earthy tones and texture. Ideal for facades and garden walls, it must be sealed and maintained properly due to its calcium content which makes it vulnerable to acidic solutions.

Quartzite: Beauty and Strength

Quartzite is a dense metamorphic rock born from sandstone, combining marble-like aesthetics with granite-like durability. UV resistant and scratch proof, it is perfect for outdoor cladding, kitchens, and floors that require sophistication and strength.
