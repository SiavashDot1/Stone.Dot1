Marble: Formation and Use in Construction

Marble is a metamorphic rock formed when limestone is subjected to high pressure and temperature. It is prized for its elegant appearance and smooth texture, often featuring beautiful veining patterns.

Positive points: Durable, aesthetic appeal, excellent for flooring, countertops, and decorative features.

Negative points: Porous and susceptible to staining, requires regular sealing, can be prone to scratching and acid damage.

Granite: Formation and Use in Construction

Granite is an igneous rock formed by the slow crystallization of magma beneath the Earth’s surface. Known for its hardness and durability, granite is widely used in construction.

Positive points: Extremely hard and resistant to scratches, heat, and weather, ideal for kitchen countertops and outdoor applications.

Negative points: Heavier and more expensive than other stones, requires professional installation.

Limestone: Formation and Use in Construction

Limestone is a sedimentary rock primarily composed of calcium carbonate, formed from marine organisms’ remains. It is softer and more porous compared to granite and marble.

Positive points: Easy to cut and shape, warm natural appearance, good for facades and flooring in low-traffic areas.

Negative points: Susceptible to weathering and acid rain damage, not suitable for heavy-use or wet areas without sealing.

Quartzite: Formation and Use in Construction

Quartzite is a metamorphic rock formed from sandstone that has undergone high heat and pressure. It is very hard and resistant to weathering.

Positive points: Very durable, scratch resistant, and low maintenance, great for kitchen countertops and flooring.

Negative points: Limited color variety, can be more expensive, requires professional installation due to hardness.
