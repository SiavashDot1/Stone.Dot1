Stone Insights Blog

Marble in Construction

Marble is formed from limestone under intense heat and pressure, resulting in a dense, elegant stone known for its classic veining. It’s often used in upscale bathrooms, floors, and sculptures. While beautiful, it is porous and softer, requiring sealing and gentle cleaning.

Granite: Strength and Style

Granite is an igneous stone known for its density and resistance to wear. Its interlocking crystal structure makes it ideal for high-traffic areas like kitchens and public buildings. Available in many colors, it’s low maintenance but can be heavy to install.

Limestone’s Versatility

Limestone, a sedimentary stone made from marine organisms, is soft and easily shaped. It’s commonly found in facades, flooring, and garden pathways. Though elegant, it’s sensitive to acids and moisture unless properly sealed.

Quartzite’s Luxury Appeal

Quartzite originates from sandstone and undergoes a metamorphic transformation that makes it incredibly hard and glossy. It mimics the look of marble with the strength of granite. Used in high-end surfaces, it’s durable but more expensive to cut and install.
