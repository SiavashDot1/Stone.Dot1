Stone Insights Blog

Marble in Architecture

Marble is a metamorphic rock known for its luxury and timeless appeal. Formed from limestone under high pressure and heat, it is available in stunning colors and veins. Ideal for interiors, it needs sealing and regular maintenance due to its porous and softer nature. Commonly used in flooring, countertops, wall cladding, and sculptures, marble adds an unmatched level of sophistication to any project.

Granite: The Durable Stone

Granite is a coarse-grained igneous rock, extremely durable, resistant to heat, scratches, and stains. It’s ideal for both interior and exterior use, especially in kitchen countertops, flooring, monuments, and building facades. It requires minimal maintenance and comes in a variety of finishes and patterns, making it versatile and long-lasting.

Limestone’s Natural Appeal

Limestone forms in marine environments from accumulated organic materials. It provides a warm, earthy look and is suitable for facades, paving, and garden decoration. However, due to its high porosity and calcium content, it is more sensitive to acid rain and cleaning agents. Sealing is essential for protection and durability.

Quartzite: The Luxury Performer

Quartzite is a naturally strong metamorphic rock that originated as sandstone. It has the appearance of marble but is harder and more resistant. It’s excellent for high-traffic areas, kitchen surfaces, and feature walls. Quartzite is UV resistant, making it a superior choice for both indoor and outdoor applications in luxurious projects.
