Stone Insights Blog

Marble in Architecture

Marble is a timeless material with elegant veining and luxurious feel. Formed through metamorphism of limestone, marble's softness makes it perfect for sculptures and interiors. It adds value to homes and buildings but requires regular sealing and maintenance due to its porous nature.

Granite: Strength Meets Style

Granite is a coarse-grained igneous rock composed mainly of quartz and feldspar. Its durability and resistance to wear, heat, and chemicals make it a top choice for countertops and exterior cladding. Available in a variety of textures, it’s both practical and visually appealing.

Quartzite: Nature’s Hard Luxury

Quartzite forms when quartz-rich sandstone is altered through heat and pressure. It looks like marble but is far harder, making it suitable for surfaces that need toughness and elegance. Quartzite is excellent for both interior and exterior high-traffic areas.

Limestone: Earthy and Elegant

Limestone is a sedimentary rock used for centuries in construction. Its muted tones offer a calm, warm aesthetic. However, its calcium carbonate content makes it sensitive to acids and environmental wear, so sealing and gentle care are essential.
