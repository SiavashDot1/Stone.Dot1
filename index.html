Stone Insights Blog

Marble in Construction

Marble is a metamorphic rock formed when limestone is subjected to high pressure and temperature. It features elegant veining and is favored for luxury interiors, sculptures, and high-end buildings. While its aesthetic appeal is unmatched, it can be prone to etching and requires sealing.

Granite: Strength and Style

Granite is an igneous rock known for its toughness and grainy appearance. It’s ideal for countertops, flooring, and exterior cladding. Its advantages include durability and heat resistance, though it’s heavier and more difficult to install.

Limestone’s Versatility

Limestone, a sedimentary rock, forms in shallow marine waters. It’s often used in façades, flooring, and landscaping. Though affordable and workable, it's porous and less durable than other stones, requiring regular maintenance.

Quartzite’s Luxury Appeal

Quartzite begins as sandstone and transforms under heat and pressure into a hard, glossy surface. It’s becoming popular in luxury interiors for countertops and wall treatments. It’s highly durable and resistant to heat and scratches, though cost and fabrication complexity are higher.
