Stone Insights Blog

Marble in Architecture

Marble is formed through the metamorphosis of limestone under intense heat and pressure. Renowned for its luxurious patterns and rich color variations, marble is used in flooring, cladding, sculptures, and countertops. Despite its beauty, it requires sealing and careful maintenance due to its softness and porosity.

Granite: Durability in Design

Granite is an igneous rock known for its durability and resistance to heat, scratches, and weathering. Commonly used in countertops, facades, and monuments, granite is a go-to for projects demanding long-lasting performance with elegant visual appeal.

Limestone and Its Warm Aesthetic

Limestone is a sedimentary rock with soft, neutral tones ideal for exteriors, flooring, and decorative features. It adds warmth but is sensitive to acidic cleaning products, so sealing is essential for long-term beauty and durability.

Quartzite: Nature’s Luxury

Quartzite originates from sandstone and is prized for its marble-like look with superior hardness. It withstands UV, weather, and abrasion, making it suitable for kitchens, bathrooms, exteriors, and designer accents that require both beauty and strength.
