Marble Formation & Uses

Marble is a metamorphic rock formed from limestone under heat and pressure. It's prized in construction for its elegance and durability but can be prone to scratching and staining if untreated.

Granite Formation & Uses

Granite is an igneous rock formed from cooled magma deep within the Earth. It is incredibly hard and resistant to weathering, making it ideal for countertops and flooring. However, it can be expensive and heavy to install.

Limestone Formation & Uses

Limestone is a sedimentary rock mainly composed of calcium carbonate. It is commonly used in building facades and flooring. While it offers aesthetic appeal, limestone is softer and more porous, requiring maintenance to avoid damage.

Quartzite Formation & Uses

Quartzite is a hard metamorphic rock originating from sandstone. It offers excellent durability and resistance to heat, perfect for kitchen surfaces. However, it may be pricier and harder to source than other stones.
