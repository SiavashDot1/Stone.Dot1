Stone Insights Blog

Marble in Architecture

Marble forms through the metamorphosis of limestone and is admired for elegance and classic aesthetics. It's used in interiors, columns, bathrooms, and high-end facades. Regular sealing is needed due to its porosity and it scratches easier than granite. However, it offers unparalleled refinement in architectural detailing.

Granite: The Durable Choice

Granite is an igneous stone valued for its strength, durability, and variety. It's ideal for kitchens, outdoor cladding, monuments, and high-traffic floors. Granite resists heat and scratches well, and its polished finish can last decades with minimal maintenance.

Why Limestone Inspires

Limestone offers warm tones and is often used in classical architecture. It requires care against acid and is more porous than granite. Ideal for patios, walls, and facades where aesthetics outweigh toughness.

Quartzite: Natural Luxury

Quartzite is sandstone turned into a dense, beautiful rock. With marble-like looks and granite-like toughness, it’s becoming the go-to for luxury countertops, walls, and floors. UV resistance makes it perfect for exteriors too.
