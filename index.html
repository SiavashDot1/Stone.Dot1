Stone Insights Blog

Marble in Construction

Marble is a metamorphic rock formed from limestone under intense heat and pressure. It is renowned for its luxurious appearance and is commonly used in high-end interiors and sculptures. However, marble is softer than other stones and may require regular sealing and maintenance.

Granite: Strength and Style

Granite is one of the hardest natural stones available, formed from cooled magma. Its durability and resistance to scratching and heat make it ideal for kitchens, bathrooms, and building exteriors. Despite its benefits, it can be heavy and may require professional installation.

Limestone’s Versatility

Limestone is a sedimentary rock composed primarily of calcium carbonate. It is often used in architecture and landscape design due to its workability and natural appearance. While beautiful, limestone is porous and may not be suitable for areas with heavy water exposure unless properly treated.

Quartzite’s Luxury Appeal

Quartzite is a hard, metamorphic rock originating from sandstone. It combines the aesthetic of marble with the strength of granite. This makes it a popular choice for countertops and decorative applications. Its main disadvantage is the cost and complexity of cutting and shaping due to its hardness.
